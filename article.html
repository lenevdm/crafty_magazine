The Age of the Mask

From strictly functional to extremely extra, 2020 ushered in a new epoch for fashion accessories: Welcome to the age of the face mask.

Before March 2020 the only place most people in South Africa saw face masks was on the weekly episode of Grey's Anatomy on TV, or at the dentist's office. From our knowledgable vantage point in February 2021, we can have a chuckle at how our world was about to change.

A few weeks after South Africa's strict 3-week lockdown period kicked off, the President kindly requested that citizens where fabric face masks in public, and this request was eventually gazetted into law.

Caught by surprise in the early days of compulsory mask-wearing, many South African's had to make due with what was available: dish towels, scarves, sports buffs, or even old t-shirts. But soon our nation's flair for self-expression took over, and today the face mask has become part of many individuals' identity signalling.

From the purely functional disposable surgical face mask that says "I don't mind spending way too much time in the pharmacy queue" to the extremely funky shwe-shwe mask that is carefully colour-coordinated with the outfit, many South Africans now enjoy expressing their personalities through their face masks.
